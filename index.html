DAROK NEWS & EVENTS 📰

Social Media Strategy Kick-off
 

The Social Media and Newsletter team met in late February to lay the groundwork for a strategy to drive interest in DAROK and galvanize voters from now through Election Day. The plan is divided into three phases:

Phase 1: Increase visibility and social media presence, publish candid, interview-style videos to connect with audiences, share voting information through reels and cards, and direct advertising through Instagram to boost our reach.

Phase 2: Event Promotion and online training, promote town hall-style events across our chapters in Seoul, Busan, and Daegu, while building new chapters in Pyeongtaek and Daejeon. Share links and details for Democrats Abroad volunteer training sessions.

Phase 3: Get Out the Vote weekly updates on voter registration deadlines, state-specific requirements, and other key information for overseas voters.

MAILING YOUR BALLOT ✉️

How to Submit Your Ballot from Korea

Make sure you are registered to vote in 2026–it only takes five minutes! Visit VoteFromAbroad.org to check your registration, register if needed, and to submit a Federal Post Card Application (FPCA) to request your absentee ballot. It is good practice to check your registration and submit a new FPCA every year.

To vote by mail, you will need two envelopes:

Outer envelope:
U.S. Embassy Seoul
188 Sejong-daero, Jongno-gu, Seoul 03141
Attention: American Citizen Services (Ballot)

Inner envelope:
Addressed to your local state election office using the postage-paid label, with your completed ballot enclosed. Seal the inner envelope, place it inside the outer envelope, and mail the package to the Embassy.

Transit time: Normal transit from Seoul to the U.S. is 7–10 business days.
