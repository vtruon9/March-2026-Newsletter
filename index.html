Social Media Strategy Kick-off
 

The Social Media and Newsletter team met in late February to lay the groundwork for a strategy to drive interest in DAROK and galvanize voters from now through Election Day. The plan is divided into three phases:

Phase 1: Increase visibility and social media presence
Publish candid, interview-style videos to connect with audiences, share voting information through reels and cards, and direct advertising through Instagram to boost our reach.

Phase 2: Event promotion and online training
Promote town hall-style events across our chapters in Seoul, Busan, and Daegu, while building new chapters in Pyeongtaek and Daejeon. Share links and details for Democrats Abroad volunteer training sessions.

Phase 3: Get Out the Vote
Weekly updates on voter registration deadlines, state-specific requirements, and other key information for overseas voters.

MAILING YOUR BALLOT 📬

Registering to Vote
 
Make sure you are registered to vote in 2026—it only takes five minutes. Visit VoteFromAbroad.org to check your registration, register if needed, and submit a Federal Post Card Application (FPCA) to request your absentee ballot.
 
Local Elections
 
In most cases you can mail your ballot through the Korean Post Office, but rules vary by state. Check your state's specific guidelines at VoteFromAbroad.org to confirm.
 
Federal Elections
 
For Federal Elections, you may send your ballot either through the Korean Post Office international shipping, or via the U.S. Embassy.
To vote by mail via the U.S., you will need two envelopes:

Outer envelope:
U.S. Embassy Seoul
188 Sejong-daero, Jongno-gu, Seoul 03141
Attention: American Citizen Services (Ballot)

Inner envelope:
Addressed to your local state election office using the postage-paid label, with your completed ballot enclosed. Seal the inner envelope, place it inside the outer envelope, and mail the package to the Embassy.

Transit time: Normal transit from Seoul to the U.S. is 7–10 business days. However, state ballot deadlines vary. Check your state's deadline and send your ballot well in advance to ensure it arrives on time.
